Could online sales take over retail one day?

Average number of online purchases by individual in Europe for 2020

UK customers are the No.1 online spenders in Europe for 2020 by purchasing more online products (92) on average than any other country, according to Eurostat's survey on a sample of online shopping platforms' customers in 33 European Countries.

Online Spending Habits

Office for National Statistics examined the number of online purchases made by UK residents in a monthly basis by taking a survey in 2020. The sample consisted of people representing different age groups from 18 to 65+ years old.

The majority of UK customers answered that they are making more than 11 purchases per month, while only 16% of the sample are ordering online products once or twice a month.

Where does e-commerce stand compared to retail at the moment?

Online vs. In-Store Retail Sales for 2006-2020

Online’s share of total retail sales has steadily been on the rise, reaching a climax of 19.2% in February of 2020, according to ONS Statistics. That’s up from 18% in 2019 and 16.3% in 2018. Note that those are pre-pandemic collected data, which help us get a objectve point of view.
During 2020, online retail sales climbed up to 36.3% over total retail in November with an average of 32.5% through-out the year, due to Covid-19 consiquences .

Comparing growth: Internet sales vs. Total retail year-on-year changes (%)

The trend of both statistics imply maintaining this pattern in the future, proving that online sales has the potential to grow even bigger, while in-store sales are estimated to expand in a more conservative manner. Additionally, it is shown that online sales growth reached a peak of 57.2% growth from 2019 to 2020 due to the pandemic’s outbreak, when all product purchases was required to take place online during lockdowns, followed by a slight decline in growth for the first time since 2008, when things came back to normal.

Estimations about Retail in the near Future

Online vs. In-Store Retail Sales for 2020-2025

Online’s share of total retail sales is estimated to continue increasing for every year from 2021 to 2025. However, those slight changes are far from reaching the 50% barrier to take over in-store retail.

E-commerce revenue forecast 2020-2025

Since 2020 online sales has been on a non-stop rise and this trend is expected to continue until 2025. The estimated increase of roughly 10% from 97 to 119.1 billion dollars in a five year period shows a promising potential in the field of online sales in United Kingdom.

United Kingdom will become the first European country to record online clothing sales overtaking in-store sales in 2022, three years earlier than excpected, according to the Future of the European Apparel Industry report.
